CHAPTER 4: NEURAL NETWORKS

The Backpropagation Algorithm

Backpropagation is the essence of neural net training. It is the practice of fine-tuning the weights of a neural net based on the error rate (i.e., loss) obtained in the previous epoch (i.e., iteration). Proper tuning of the weights guarantees lower error rates, making the model reliable by increasing its generalization capability.

The goals of backpropagation are straightforward:

  • Calculate the gradient of the loss function with respect to the weights.
  • Update the weights using gradient descent or a related optimization algorithm.
